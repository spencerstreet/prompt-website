— What it is

A soft tool for turning loose plans — a Sunday picnic, drinks after work, a 30th — into real, attended events.

No feed. No followers. No follower count. It reads like a text from the friend organising the night, not a marketing email about it.

— What's inside

Plans, not posts.

01

A warm list of invitations.

Your home screen is people you actually know, sending you things you might actually go to. That's it.

02

Real event detail.

Host, date, location with map, comments, photos. Everything you'd text about, in one place.

03

Quiet by default.

No feed to refresh, no notifications for things you didn't ask for. If you don't add friends, the app is silent.

— Small print

Quick questions.

01
Is it free?
Yes. Free to download, free to use. The beta is for invited testers via Apple's TestFlight programme — there's a limited number of seats while we're still polishing.
02
iOS only?
For now, yes. iOS 17+ on iPhone. Android and a desktop view are on the roadmap — but we're focused on getting the iPhone experience right first.
03
Will my friends see what I'm up to?
Only the ones you invite. There's no feed, no public profile, no follower count. If you don't add anyone, the app is quiet.
04
When's it out properly?
Soon. The beta is where we sharpen the rough edges. Join, send a few invites, tell us what you think.
